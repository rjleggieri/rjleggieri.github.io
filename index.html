01
Text-Prosody AlignmentCase Studies in Machine Learning · UT Austin

The "I'm Fine" Effect

When the words say one thing and the voice says another.

I designed and evaluated the Alignment Discrepancy Score (ADS), an interpretable measure of the distance between text-derived sentiment and audio-derived prosody in a shared valence-arousal space.

Data189 DAIC-WOZ clinical interviews
Unit of analysisUtterance-level alignment
Evaluation5-fold participant-level cross-validation
Observed result≈0.62 cross-validated AUC; 0.49 audio-only; ≈0.70 text-only

What the result showed: ADS did not sharply separate PHQ groups. The PHQ-elevated group instead showed a tighter distribution with fewer high-ADS outliers, consistent with reduced emotional range. A threshold selected for case detection achieved ≈0.80 sensitivity; a balanced threshold yielded ≈0.74 sensitivity and ≈0.51 specificity.

Scope: Sentiment-ADS achieved approximately 0.62 cross-validated AUC, above audio-only at 0.49 but below text-only at approximately 0.70. Its value is not superior classification: ADS exposes cross-modal variability that a text-only prediction cannot show. It is best understood as an interpretable exploratory cue, not a replacement for text features or a diagnostic tool.

Public follow-on

text-prosody-evidence ↗ turns lessons from the research into a dataset-neutral reference implementation for leakage-aware evaluation, calibration, reliability, and auditable evidence. It is a stricter engineering direction, not a reproduction or validation of the original findings.

NLP Final ProjectDeBERTa-v3-base · MNLI · HANS

Dataset cartography for robust NLI

Applied established dataset-cartography methods to 392,702 MNLI examples across five epochs, mapping each example by confidence, variability, and correctness. This was an application of existing methods to a different model and dataset setting, not a claim of methodological novelty.

The best configuration down-weighted both easy and hard examples. It improved HANS adversarial accuracy from 77.49% to 78.59% while retaining 88.47% MNLI validation accuracy; attempts to emphasize hard examples consistently hurt performance, pointing to possible label-quality issues in that region.
