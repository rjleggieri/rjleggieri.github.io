Text–Prosody AlignmentCase Studies in Machine Learning · UT Austin

The “I’m fine” effect.

When the words say one thing and the voice says another.

I designed and evaluated the Alignment Discrepancy Score (ADS), an interpretable measure of the distance between text-derived sentiment and audio-derived prosody in a shared valence–arousal space.

DataDAIC-WOZ clinical interviews
Unit of analysisUtterance-level alignment
EvaluationElevated PHQ-8 classification
Observed result≈0.62 AUC; above audio-only baselines

Scope: Exploratory research prototype—not a diagnostic tool. The modest signal requires external validation before any clinical interpretation.

Public follow-on

Graduate NLP projectDeBERTa-v3-base · MNLI · HANS

Dataset cartography for robust NLI

Logged per-example training dynamics for 392,702 MNLI examples across five epochs, mapping each example by confidence, variability, and correctness. Those categories drove weighted cross-entropy through a custom PyTorch dataset and Hugging Face Trainer.

The best configuration down-weighted both easy and hard examples. It improved HANS adversarial accuracy from 77.49% to 78.59% while retaining 88.47% MNLI validation accuracy; attempts to emphasize hard examples consistently hurt performance, pointing to possible label-quality issues in that region.

Map 392K examples→Re-weight loss by difficulty→Evaluate on 30K HANS examples
